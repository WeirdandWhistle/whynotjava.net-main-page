TLS 1.3 is the backbone of the encrypted internet. Ever hear of SSL? Neither have I but TLS is the successor to that and the latest version is 1.3. SSL 1.0 was made by Taher Elgamal but was never published, SSL 2.0 came out in early 1995 but contained many security flaws. SSL 3.0 got published to replace SSL 2.0, TLS 1.0 (1999) upgraded SSL 3.0. TLS 1.1 (April 2006) updated flaws in its predecessor, TLS 1.2 (August 2008) was based on 1.1 but had mayjor differences. TLS 1.0 and TLS 1.1 were deprecated in March 2021. RFC 8446 "The Transport Layer Security (TLS) Protocol Version 1.3" (August 2018) would finaly put all its predecessor (SSL 1-3, TLS 1.0-2) to rest. Though TLS 1.2 is stilled used it's being obsoleted and countines to get older and more outdated every day. But so? What do all these numbers and letters mean? As mentioned they are the backbone of modern internet encryption. Anytime you goto a webpage (that is not old) you connect via an internet protocol called HTTPS -> HTTP-S -> HTTP Secure -> HTTP over TLS (originally HTTP over SSL). Take https://google.com as an example. When you goto to google your browser prefroms a small, instant, invisible, handshake that agrees on a secret big number. A REALLY REALLY BIG number. This number is used in conjuction with other cryptographic protcols that jumble your infromation into a unreadable mess unless you have the secret big number.
